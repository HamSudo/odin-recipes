Cobb Salad

Description

Cobb salad is a hearty, American-style salad known for its colorful presentation and rich combination of ingredients. Traditionally served as a main course, it features chopped greens—usually romaine, iceberg lettuce, or watercress—topped with neatly arranged rows of diced chicken breast, crispy bacon, hard-boiled eggs, avocado, tomatoes, blue cheese, and chives. The ingredients are typically laid out in stripes or sections on top of the greens rather than mixed together, giving the salad its signature composed look.

The salad is usually dressed with a red wine vinaigrette, though variations may use ranch, blue cheese, or other dressings. Originating in the 1930s at the Brown Derby restaurant in Hollywood, the Cobb salad was reportedly created by the restaurant's owner, Robert Cobb, as a late-night kitchen improvisation. Its balance of protein, healthy fats, and fresh vegetables makes it both satisfying and nutritious, offering a filling meal that feels both indulgent and wholesome.

Ingredients

  • 6 slices bacon
  • 1 head iceberg lettuce, shredded
  • 3 cups chopped, cooked chicken meat
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, seeded and chopped
  • 3/4 cup blue cheese, crumpled
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 1 avocado-peeled, pitted and diced
  • 1 (8 ounce) bottle Ranch-style salad dressing

Steps

  1. Place eggs in a saucepan and cover completely with cold water; bring to a boil, then cover and remove from heat. Let eggs sit for 10 to 12 minutes, then cool, peel and chop.
  2. While the eggs are cooking, place bacon in a large, deep skillet. Cook over medium-high heat until evenly brown, 7 to 10 minutes. Drain, crumble, and set aside.
  3. Divide shredded lettuce among individual plates. Arrange rows of bacon, eggs, chicken, tomatoes, blue cheese, green onions, and avocado on top.
  4. Drizzle with dressing and enjoy!
